Music Therapy

Methodology

Diaphragmatic Breathing

Participants were guided to focus on present bodily sensations by closing their eyes and breathing deeply in and out to the phrase “I am here now.”

Vocal Training

Repetition of vowels such as “ah,” “ee,” and “oo” encouraged participants to express and release emotions through sound.

Singing Familiar Songs

Participants sang familiar tunes—including Korean popular songs, children’s songs, and hymns—using lyrics as a means of reclaiming aspects of their identity.

Rhythmic Movement

Simple movements such as clapping and foot-tapping performed to music helped participants gradually overcome initial awkwardness and experience a sense of unity

Correlation

Music therapy has been utilised for centuries as a complementary and alternative intervention for various neuropsychiatric disorders, including Parkinson’s disease, anxiety, depression, insomnia, dementia, autism, and pain syndromes [17].

Contemporary research supports its ability to stimulate neuroplasticity and support cognitive, emotional, and social functioning [18]. It is frequently used in conjunction with psychotherapy and other expressive therapies.
