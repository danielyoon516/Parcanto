About Parkinson’s

Parkinson’s disease (PD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder characterised by the degeneration of dopamine-producing neurons in the substantia nigra, leading to dopamine deficiency that disrupts neuronal signalling and manifests in both motor and non-motor symptoms [1]. While PD is widely recognised for motor features—bradykinesia, resting tremor, rigidity, and postural instability—it also significantly affects speech and communication. Early in the disease course, individuals often experience disrupted speech rhythm, reduced vocal volume (hypophonia), and impaired articulation, which can erode their sense of identity and contribute to social withdrawal before prominent motor symptoms appear [2][3]
